What caused the two peaks?
Bitcoin Peak
~$46k–69k  (2021)
  • 2020 3rd Halving (May 2020) Block reward cut from 12.5 to 6.25 BTC, tightening supply at a time of surging demand.
  • 2020 COVID-era money printing Massive global stimulus and near-zero interest rates drove investors toward inflation hedges. Bitcoin absorbed a large share of that capital flight.
  • 2020 MicroStrategy & Tesla MicroStrategy purchased over $1B in BTC. Tesla followed with $1.5B. Corporate treasury adoption legitimized Bitcoin as an institutional asset for the first time.
  • 2021 DeFi & NFT boom Explosive growth of decentralized finance protocols and NFT markets funneled massive speculative capital into the entire crypto ecosystem, lifting BTC.
  • 2021 El Salvador legal tender In September 2021 El Salvador adopted Bitcoin as legal tender — the first country to do so — providing a major sentiment boost and symbolic legitimacy.
  • 2021 First Bitcoin Futures ETF (USA) ProShares' Bitcoin futures ETF listed on the NYSE in October 2021, opening the door to mainstream investors and triggering the final push toward $69k.
Ukraine Aid Peak
~$105B  (2023)
  • Feb 2022 Full-scale Russian invasion Russia launched a large-scale ground invasion on 24 February 2022, triggering an immediate and unprecedented international response. Aid exploded from ~$3B to ~$93B in a single year.
  • 2022 US Congress emergency packages The US passed four aid bills totalling ~$113B across 2022–2023, by far the largest single-country contributor. Packages covered military hardware, direct budget support and humanitarian relief.
  • 2022 NATO & EU collective mobilisation NATO allies shed long-held reluctance to supply heavy weapons. The EU activated the European Peace Facility to fund lethal aid for the first time in its history.
  • 2023 EU €18B Macro-Financial Assistance+ The European Commission issued EU-Bonds to raise €18B for Ukraine's budget, disbursing the full amount by December 2023 to keep the Ukrainian state functioning under wartime conditions.
  • 2023 ATACMS long-range missiles approved The Biden administration approved transfer of long-range precision missiles capable of striking targets ~200 miles inside Russian territory — a major escalation in aid quality.
  • 2023 F-16 transfer green-lit In summer 2023, the US allowed European allies to supply US-made F-16 fighter jets — marking the transition to supplying modern Western air power.
